Our Philosophy

Slow fashion, elegance, and artistry at the heart of everything we create.

At Fashion Box we believe in thoughtful design and enduring beauty. Our work is guided by a reverence for craft — each garment carries the mark of human touch, intention, and restraint. We prioritise quality over quantity, seasonless silhouettes over fleeting trends, and partnerships that uplift artisans and their communities. This is our commitment to slow fashion: fewer, better-made pieces that invite care, repair and stories that last.

Fashion Timeline

Major revolutions and milestones that shaped global fashion — plus a dedicated timeline for India.

  1. 18th–19th Century
    Birth of haute couture — Paris becomes the epicenter of bespoke fashion and seasonal collections.
  2. 1920s
    Modern ready-to-wear emerges and silhouettes shift to freer, functional forms; mass production begins to influence style.
  3. 1960s
    Youth culture revolution — street style, mini-skirts and subcultural looks reshape mainstream fashion.
  4. 1980s–90s
    Designer branding & globalization — luxury houses expand worldwide; fashion weeks and media amplify trends.
  5. 2000s–Present
    Digital & sustainable turns — e-commerce, social media, and slow-fashion movements redefine production and consumption.
  1. Ancient – Medieval
    Textile traditions — regional weaving, dyeing and embroidery establish a rich sartorial heritage (sari, dhoti, regional crafts).
  2. Mughal Era
    Luxury textiles & courtly styles — brocades, zari and intricate motifs flourish under royal patronage.
  3. Colonial Period
    Hybrid styles — Western tailoring and Indian textiles intersect, changing urban dress codes.
  4. 1990s
    Industry growth after liberalization — Indian designers and fashion weeks gain prominence; contemporary industry accelerates.
  5. 2000s–Present
    Global recognition & craft revival — designers blend heritage with modernity; sustainability and artisanal partnerships rise.
